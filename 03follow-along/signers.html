Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence (1776) include

Each of these individuals are well known not just for signing the document, but for their broader roles in founding the United States.

Thomas Jefferson (Virginia)
Primary author of the Declaration of Independence. Later served as the 3rd President of the United States and articulated core ideas about natural rights and government by consent.
John Adams (Massachusetts)
Leading advocate for independence in the Continental Congress. Later became the 2nd President of the United States and played a key role in persuading others to support independence.
Benjamin Franklin (Pennsylvania)
Renowned diplomat, scientist, and political thinker. Helped edit the Declaration and was crucial in securing French support during the Revolutionary War.
John Hancock (Massachusetts)
President of the Continental Congress at the time of signing. Famous for his large, bold signature, which became synonymous with the word “signature.”
Samuel Adams (Massachusetts)
Influential political organizer and propagandist who helped mobilize public support for independence well before 1776.
Richard Henry Lee (Virginia)
Introduced the Lee Resolution, which formally proposed independence and initiated the process that led to the Declaration of Independence.
Roger Sherman (Connecticut)
The only person to sign all four major founding documents: the Continental Association, the Declaration of Independence, the Articles of Confederation, and the U.S. Constitution.
Robert Morris (Pennsylvania)
Principal financier of the Revolutionary War, later known as the “Financier of the Revolution.”
Charles Carroll of Carrollton (Maryland)
One of the wealthiest colonists and the only Catholic signer of the Declaration. Lived long enough to see the United States firmly established.
